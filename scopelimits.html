Scope and Limits

It was important to define the scope and limits of the project as the aim of PhishNet involved complex and advanced programming techniques and integration. Furthermore, user interaction was another crucial factor to the success of the project. The obvious limitations to PhishNet being rolled out as a fully functional application were time and money, as both factors would impact the quality and quantity of work along with the available resources for research and development. Another factor that would impact the deliverables of the project was the available skills of each team member and their capabilities to achieve milestones using their prior experiences and knowledge rather than taking the time to learn new skillsets. It became evident that the more functionality we wanted to add to PhishNet, the more likely the project could enter scope creep and potentially open up further quality control issues related to security and interaction. To this end, the following definition was used to further define our scope: If a user were to ask to see the results of our work, the expectation is at minimum to provide a cloud based, visual and interactive website and application that demonstrated user interactivity and accessibility. This meant the team leant more towards a feasible goal of user access, design and interactivity via the creation of the dashboard over the risks of chasing potentially application breaking technical features.
