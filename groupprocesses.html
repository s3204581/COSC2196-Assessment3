Processes

the group was able to submit Assignment 2 on time and with all sections completed, feedback and scoring reflected poor time management and content that was either lacking substance or wasn't fully answering the topic requirements. This feedback showed that changes needed to be made to group processes, particularly with time management to improve on content and team member involvement to improve on work distribution.

The team will be introducing additional tools for assignment 3 to assist in time management, transparency and work allocation in the form of a Trello board along with additional meetings scheduled for further communication and team check-ins. Taking on an agile methodology will mean that all team members will be taking on the role of stakeholder by collaborating on what work will be undertaken in short weekly sprints and being responsible for generating tickets in a burndown for future sprint planning and moderating the backlog of incomplete sprint tickets. The intention is for each team member to be accountable for their share of the workload, completed in a timely manner so more time can be spent on reviewing, formatting and refinement before submission.

Meetings will take on a scrum format with a weekly rotating scrum master that will be responsible for preparing the weekly agenda and action plan, to be recorded for team members that were unable to attend and for final submission requirements. The rotation is intended to share the workload evenly and for each team member to experience the leadership role. This also promotes team members being fully aware of the project requirements so that the role of scrum master can be done effectively.
