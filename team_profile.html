Team Profile - The Agile Expendables

The Agile Expendables is a dynamic team comprising four members: Hugo Ekert, David Martin, Jake Harman and Igor Zofrin. As the Agile-Expendables, we embody the principles of agility and adaptability. Together, we bring a diverse range of skills, experience, and perspectives to tackle challenges and deliver results.

Hugo Ekert - s3376686

I'm based in Melbourne, and I started in IT as a manual software tester and I have gained valuable experience working within Scrum teams at various software companies. Primarily focusing on automated software testing, I have also ventured into software development, expanding my skills and knowledge. I enjoy the programming side of IT, especially in solving problems through automation and exploring new tools and frameworks. Outside of work my hobbies are playing Futsal and I like to review movies on Letterboxd.

Career Plans

My career plan is to work as a DevOps engineer. Having witnessed firsthand the transformative impact of effective DevOps practices on organizational productivity and development team empowerment, I am passionate about contributing to this field. However, I have also observed that poorly implemented DevOps can create significant obstacles, hindering teams from accomplishing their goals. DevOps revolves around a philosophy of creating efficient workflows for development teams, requiring expertise in programming for automation and . In comparison to other career paths, DevOps shares technical aspects with Application Security (Igor) and Games Development (Ben). Particularly with a game developer a DevOps engineer would need to understand the languages and Technolgies the developer is using and support the development with pipelines for building and testing the game. Additionally, DevOps can be integrated into the Agile Delivery process (Dave). However, it differs significantly from a UX Lead's role (Ben) as it primarily focuses on supporting teams rather than directly addressing end-user experience, although a UX Lead's involvement may still be part of the overall process.

Career Plans

I personally feel that the variety of experiences and motivations that each member of the team brings to the table helps with ascertaining strengths and weaknesses and I enjoy helping to integrate this diversity of skillsets into our project requirements with Agile methodologies. While I have experience leading teams in an operational context, I am finding this project development lifecycle to be a great snapshot of working in an IT environment with a team to solve complex problems and brainstorm unique solutions and ideas. I feel that an Agile delivery lead requires the experiences from a variety of project elements and phases along with needing the skill to communicate with a variety of parties both outside of and within a team. Comparing this role to that of the other roles listed by the team I can identify these factors as the major differentiators amongst each ideal job. However, I see the ability to work well with others and the necessity to capture ideas and capitalise on the varied experiences of others to achieve a goal being the major common element for each person on this team.

Career Plans

While a UX Lead appears to differ significantly from the other careers in our group, there still remains crossover. Production of game development will utilise a lot of similar tenets relating to a user-centric approach applied in different ways to achieve different outcomes. Game development and UX both rely on research and testing to achieve more data-driven design choices, and to validate design decisions. Conversely, careers in DevOps and Platform security have a much greater emphasis on back-end systems and may limited to no similarities to work within UX. Agile IT Delivery Leads have commonality with UX Leads with respect to aligning business goals with stakeholders, and team management including things like efficiency, effectiveness, mentorship, motivation and correct handling of team dynamics.

Career Plans

My career plan is to become a Cyber Security Analyst specializing in forensic analysis and incident response. Historically, this role type has not been an easily accessible role to step into or progress given the specialized skills and knowledge required which are typically provided only via expensive courses and work experience. However, as significance of Information security industry grows by the day, online presence of information shared by industry professionals is more readily available and new study courses are becoming available to the public, it is possible to gain initial understanding and skills for entry level roles. I have always had passion for Cyber Security since being introduced to computers as there is always have room to grow new skills and understanding in modern technologies and was lucky to be introduced into this entry level role. In our project, Cyber Security analytical and engineering skills will compliment configuration and continuous deployment (Hugo - DevOps) to cooperatively configure secure web platform and maintaining software supply chain validation and application security controls for software development (Ben - developer)
