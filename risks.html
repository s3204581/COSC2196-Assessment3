Risks

The team opted to implement a Risk Register to not only catalogue any identifiable risks throughout the project lifecycle but to also act as a repository to plan mitigation actions, contingencies and map progress on actions to lower both risk and likelihood. The risk and likelihood were measured on a gauge of low, medium or high to serve as a severity matrix which the team could use to escalate and prioritise the risks that were most likely to impact the project's success. Team members were encouraged to record any perceived risks regardless of nature – be it hardware, team or work related – and the process of determining a priority based on a severity matrix guaranteed focus was placed on risks that were specific and meaningful to the project.

An example of a high risk based on severity was the chance that the project description was not well defined which could impact the entire process of implementation. The mitigating action for this risk was to design wireframes based on use cases so that a developed prototype could provide further clarification. The importance of getting this completed due to time constraints meant that this risk needed to be addressed or else time may not have permitted the contingency plan of shifting the project to a more easily defined option.
